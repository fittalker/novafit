Sources & Citations

Where NovaFit gets its information. Every recommendation Nova makes is grounded in the following established guidelines and peer-reviewed research.

Daily Maintenance Calories
Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

Used to estimate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). Widely considered the most accurate predictive equation for estimating resting metabolic rate in healthy individuals.

Mifflin MD, St Jeor ST, et al. "A new predictive equation for resting energy expenditure in healthy individuals." American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 1990. PubMed

Harris-Benedict Equation (Revised)

Used as a secondary reference for BMR estimation and activity multiplier calculations.

Roza AM, Shizgal HM. "The Harris Benedict equation reevaluated." American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 1984. PubMed

Workout Routines & Programming
ACSM Guidelines for Exercise Testing and Prescription

The foundation for NovaFit's workout programming, including rep ranges, set volumes, rest periods, and progressive overload structures.

American College of Sports Medicine. ACSM's Guidelines for Exercise Testing and Prescription, 11th Edition. acsm.org

NSCA Essentials of Strength Training and Conditioning

Referenced for exercise selection, movement mechanics, training frequency, and periodization principles used in the 180-day program.

National Strength and Conditioning Association. Essentials of Strength Training and Conditioning, 4th Edition. nsca.com

Rest Days & Recovery
ACSM Position Stand on Progression Models

Guides NovaFit's rest day scheduling and recovery recommendations, including minimum recovery periods between resistance training sessions for the same muscle groups.

ACSM. "Progression Models in Resistance Training for Healthy Adults." Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise, 2009. PubMed

Dietary & Nutrition Information
WHO Healthy Diet Guidelines

Referenced for general dietary recommendations, macronutrient balance, and daily intake guidelines.

World Health Organization. "Healthy Diet Fact Sheet." who.int

ISSN Position Stand on Protein and Exercise

The basis for NovaFit's protein intake recommendations relative to body weight and training goals.

Jager R, et al. "International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: protein and exercise." Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2017. PubMed

ISSN Position Stand on Diets and Body Composition

Referenced for guidance on caloric deficits, surplus ranges, and macronutrient distribution for body recomposition.

Aragon AA, et al. "International society of sports nutrition position stand: diets and body composition." JISSN, 2017. PubMed

Physical Activity Guidelines
WHO Physical Activity Guidelines

Used to frame minimum activity thresholds and weekly exercise volume recommendations within the app.

World Health Organization. "WHO guidelines on physical activity and sedentary behaviour." 2020. who.int

Nova AI Coach
How Nova generates recommendations

Nova's responses are generated using a large language model that has been instructed to ground its fitness and nutrition advice in the guidelines and research listed on this page. Nova is designed to provide general fitness guidance and motivation. It does not diagnose medical conditions, prescribe treatments, or replace professional advice from a certified trainer, dietitian, or physician.

Streaks & Consistency
Habit Formation Research

NovaFit's streak and XP systems are informed by behavioral science research on habit formation and consistency.

Lally P, et al. "How are habits formed: Modelling habit formation in the real world." European Journal of Social Psychology, 2010. PubMed

Important

NovaFit is a fitness companion, not a medical device. The information provided in this app is for general educational and motivational purposes only.
